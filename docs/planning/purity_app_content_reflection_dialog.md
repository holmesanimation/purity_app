# Purity App — Content Reflection Dialog

## Core Purpose

The Content Reflection Dialog is intended to help the user grow in:
- wisdom,
- discernment,
- self-awareness,
- and spiritual vigilance

WITHOUT:
- shame,
- obsessive self-analysis,
- fear-based legalism,
- or compulsive introspection.

The dialog should feel:
- calm,
- encouraging,
- reflective,
- and pastoral.

It is NOT:
- a “failure report,”
- a trigger audit,
- or a moral scoring system.

The goal is:
> learn gently from experiences and build wisdom over time.

---

# Availability

The Content Reflection Dialog should be accessible:

## 1. Anytime From Main Dashboard
A button or panel:
> "Content Reflection"

This allows voluntary reflection at any time.

---

## 2. Weekly Encouragement Review
Once per week:
- optional popup/dialog
- framed positively and encouragingly
- never aggressive or guilt-inducing

The weekly review should feel like:
> a quiet spiritual check-in.

---

# Weekly Review Tone

## If No Panic Sessions Occurred

Celebrate gently.

Example copy:

> "No panic sessions this week.
>
> Praise God for His sustaining grace and continued work in your life."

Avoid:
- prideful/congratulatory tone
- streak obsession
- gamification

The emphasis should remain:
- gratitude,
- humility,
- dependence on God.

---

## If Panic Sessions Did Occur

Avoid failure language.

Example copy:

> "You used the panic button this week.
>
> That was an act of interruption and honesty.
>
> What did you learn?"

The goal:
- reinforce honesty,
- reinforce interruption behavior,
- encourage wisdom formation,
- avoid despair/shame.

- You reached out for help

- You continued fighting

- sanctification

---

# Section 2 — Content Reflection

## Prompt

> "Was there any content this week that affected your heart or mind?"

Examples:
- movie
- TV show
- YouTube
- social media
- browsing
- music
- advertisements

The user should NOT feel pressured to fill everything out.

Reflection should remain lightweight.

---

# Reflection Questions

## Optional Questions

### What content affected you?

Freeform text field.

---

### How did it affect you?

Optional text field.

Examples:
- temptation
- anxiety
- discouragement
- comparison
- anger
- lust
- numbness

---

### Were you already vulnerable?

Optional multi-select:
- tired
- lonely
- anxious
- angry
- discouraged
- avoiding something
- home alone
- unsure

Purpose:
- identify context without blame.

---

# Exposure Classification

## Reflection Classification

Prompt:

> "How would you describe the situation?"

Options:
- accidental exposure
- poor wisdom
- intentional drift
- unsure

Purpose:
- encourage discernment,
- not legalism.

The app should help distinguish:
- accidental exposure
vs
- knowingly drifting deeper.

---

# Future Wisdom Prompt

## Gentle Discernment

Prompt:

> "What wisdom do you want to carry forward from this?"

Examples:
- avoid certain content
- stop earlier
- listen to conviction sooner
- avoid browsing while exhausted
- pray sooner
- reach out earlier

Purpose:
- wisdom-building,
- not self-condemnation.

---

# Optional Future Decision

Prompt:

> "Should this content be avoided in the future?"

Options:
- yes
- maybe
- no
- unsure

This should NEVER:
- automatically blacklist content,
- shame the user,
- or trigger punitive behavior.

Purpose:
- personal discernment only.

# Tone & Emotional Design Principles

The dialog should feel:
- encouraging
- calm
- honest
- hopeful
- grace-oriented
- wisdom-oriented

It should NOT feel:
- accusatory
- obsessive
- hyper-analytical
- punitive
- fear-based
- legalistic

---

# Hyper-Analysis Avoidance

The system should avoid:
- excessive statistics
- over-monitoring behavior
- constant trigger analysis
- emotionally loaded language
- obsessive self-surveillance

The goal is:
> wisdom and awareness,
NOT:
> compulsive introspection.

# Core Philosophy

The Content Reflection Dialog exists to help the user ask:

- What weakens me?
- What strengthens me?
- What environments affect me?
- What habits produce clarity?
- When do I ignore conviction?
- What wisdom should I carry forward?

The purpose is:
> growth in wisdom and discernment.

NOT:
> fear, shame, or obsessive monitoring.
